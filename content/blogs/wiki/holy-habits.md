# Holy Habits

**Summary**: Holy habits are structured, repeatable practices that orient the Christian toward God, Kingdom identity, and participation in God's redemptive work; they are the practical form [[spiritual formation]] takes.

**Sources**: [2020-03-04-three-holy-habits.md](../raw/2020-03-04-three-holy-habits.md), [2025-08-07-holy-and-profane.md](../raw/2025-08-07-holy-and-profane.md), [2021-04-10-table-centered.md](../raw/2021-04-10-table-centered.md), [2020-03-07-a-liturgy-of-love.md](../raw/2020-03-07-a-liturgy-of-love.md), [2017-10-01-creating-unsafe-spaces.md](../raw/2017-10-01-creating-unsafe-spaces.md), [2019-10-14-the-life-gauges-through-postures.md](../raw/2019-10-14-the-life-gauges-through-postures.md)

## The Three Holy Habits

The blog's central framework for practiced spirituality is three interlocking habits (source: [2020-03-04-three-holy-habits.md](../raw/2020-03-04-three-holy-habits.md)):

### 1. Tune In
Focus attention on God — concentrate on his presence, his word, his character. The noise of modern life competes with this attentiveness, so "tuning in" is an act of deliberate reorientation. Related to the [[life gauges]] of Worship and Responsibility (cultivating a receptive posture toward God).

### 2. Enter In
Adopt Kingdom identity and culture — step into the "people like us do things like this" reality of being in Christ. This includes practicing the [[spiritual formation]] postures: recognizing who we are in Christ, rejecting contrary messages, turning toward God's truth. It is an ongoing act, not a one-time arrival.

### 3. Join In
Participate in God's redemptive work in the world — the outward movement of [[mission]]. As God's ambassadors, "we so love the world that we give." This is not added onto spiritual formation; it flows from it.

These three habits describe a movement: attention (inward/upward) → identity (transformation) → action (outward). Formation without joining in is incomplete; joining in without formation is unsustainable.

## Holy and Profane

God's primary categories are not "good/bad" or "right/wrong" but **holy** and **profane** (source: [2025-08-07-holy-and-profane.md](../raw/2025-08-07-holy-and-profane.md), citing Ezekiel 44:23):

- **Holy**: set apart to God
- **Profane**: not set apart

What is holy is always good because God himself is good. The question for all of life — relationships, time, work, speech, spaces — is: is this set apart to God, or not?

This reframes habit formation. Holy habits are those practices that set life apart to God; they are not merely useful or productive but *consecrated*.

## The Table as Holy Habit

The Lord's Table (Eucharist) is treated as the central holy habit of Christian [[community]] (source: [2021-04-10-table-centered.md](../raw/2021-04-10-table-centered.md)). The etymology of "companion" is *cum panis* — "with bread." At the table, life is exchanged: with God (thanksgiving and encounter) and with one another (*koinonia* fellowship, per 1 John 1:3).

A church centered on the table is centered on the practices that sustain communal life in Christ: intimacy, worship, discovery, and hospitality.

## Liturgy as Love

Even mundane acts — hand-washing (written during 2020's pandemic) — can be holy habits when understood as liturgical acts of love. The practice of caring for your own hygiene becomes "a gift of love to the whole world" with multiplying effects that reach family, friends, and enemies alike (source: [2020-03-07-a-liturgy-of-love.md](../raw/2020-03-07-a-liturgy-of-love.md)).

This democratizes holiness: every act done with intentional love toward God and neighbor is potentially holy, not only acts performed inside church buildings.

## Postures as Practice

The [[life gauges]] framework teaches that each spiritual posture can be physically embodied (source: [2019-10-14-the-life-gauges-through-postures.md](../raw/2019-10-14-the-life-gauges-through-postures.md)):

- **Humility**: kneel, bow, raise hands — "thoroughly defeated by overwhelming love"
- **Responsibility**: open hands receiving, then drawing close — stewardship of grace
- **Acclimation**: 180-degree turn — ongoing repentance and reorientation
- **Imitation**: open hands extending outward — generosity flowing from what was received
- **Mission**: arms wide, gathering — "corralling people to a place of acceptance and shalom"

Physical practice reinforces spiritual reality. The body learns what the mind intends.
