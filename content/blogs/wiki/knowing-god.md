## Knowing vs. Knowing About

A crucial distinction runs through many posts: the difference between knowing *about* God (intellectual information) and *knowing* God (relational experience). "The information might change your perspective. The experience will change your life." (source: [2025-05-09-god-is-love.md](../raw/2025-05-09-god-is-love.md))

This mirrors the difference between studying a person's biography and actually befriending them. Christian formation aims at the latter. Information about God without relational encounter falls short of what the blog calls eternal life.

## Eternal Life as Relationship Now

Eternal life is not primarily a future destination but a present relational reality. Drawing on John 17:3 ("This is eternal life: that they know you, the only true God"), the blog consistently identifies knowing God as the core of Christian existence — the telos beyond even [[discipleship]].

YWAM's mission statement — "To know God and make Him known" — is treated as a near-complete summary of the Christian life (source: [2025-05-19-to-know-god-and-make-him-known.md](../raw/2025-05-19-to-know-god-and-make-him-known.md)). Knowing God and making him known produce fellowship: *koinonia* with the Father and the Son, drawing others into that same fellowship.

## Discipleship's Superior

Even [[discipleship]] — training by Christ — serves a larger end: coming to know the Trainer. "The superior of discipleship is knowing God, who is the Trainer himself." (source: [2025-08-12-discipleships-superior.md](../raw/2025-08-12-discipleships-superior.md))

This frames all spiritual practices as means, not ends. [[Holy habits]], [[life gauges]], and formation structures all exist to facilitate this knowing.

## How God Communicates

God's *logos* — his persuasive reasoning and intent — reaches us through three channels (source: [2019-08-07-the-word-of-god.md](../raw/2019-08-07-the-word-of-god.md)):

1. **Nature** — Creation as God's "first book" (Psalm 19:1-6; Romans 1:19-20)
2. **Scripture** — God's written word (2 Timothy 3:16-17)
3. **Jesus** — The Word made flesh (John 1:1-18)

Each channel requires humble hearts to receive. Each can be corrupted: worshipping nature instead of God, twisting Scripture to human agendas, studying Jesus without being transformed.

## Jesus as the Interpretive Key

Because all three channels of [[knowing God]] can be misread, Jesus functions as the interpretive key for all of Scripture and all of life. "Jesus came to reveal what God is truly like and what a human being should be like." (source: [2025-06-17-the-interpretive-key.md](../raw/2025-06-17-the-interpretive-key.md))

The same biblical texts have produced both Mother Teresa and the Crusaders. A Christocentric hermeneutic — asking what Jesus reveals about God and humanity — resolves these contradictions. John 14:6 ("If you really know me, you will know my Father") and John 14:6 ("I am the way") make Jesus both the subject and the method of knowing God.

## Trained in Knowing

[[Discipleship]] as training by Christ is fundamentally epistemological before it is ethical. Jesus' own pattern was to do only what he saw the Father doing (John 5:19). Being "trained by Christ" means learning to *know* as Christ knows — aligning perception and will with the Father's — before moving to action. (source: [2025-09-15-trained-by-christ.md](../raw/2025-09-15-trained-by-christ.md))

Two groups especially need their knowing deepened (source: [2025-10-21-to-make-him-known.md](../raw/2025-10-21-to-make-him-known.md)):
- Those with incomplete or legalistic knowledge of God
- Those yearning for goodness, dignity, and justice without knowing the God who grounds it

Both are addressed through Paul's posture at the Athenian altar: "Let me introduce you to Someone you need to know, and know very well." (Acts 17:23)
