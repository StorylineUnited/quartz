# Life Gauges

**Summary**: The life gauges are a five-stage framework for [[spiritual formation]] — Humility, Responsibility, Acclimation, Imitation, and Mission — each building on the prior, with Worship as the irreducible foundation.

**Sources**: [2019-08-17-summarizing-the-life-gauges.md](../raw/2019-08-17-summarizing-the-life-gauges.md), [2019-09-26-the-life-gauges-have-a-priority.md](../raw/2019-09-26-the-life-gauges-have-a-priority.md), [2019-10-14-the-life-gauges-through-postures.md](../raw/2019-10-14-the-life-gauges-through-postures.md)

**Last updated**: 2026-04-17

## The Five Gauges

The life gauges describe five integrated dimensions of the Christian life, each with its own posture, confession, and prerequisite (source: [2019-08-17-summarizing-the-life-gauges.md](../raw/2019-08-17-summarizing-the-life-gauges.md)):

| Gauge          | Posture                                  | Confession                                    |
| -------------- | ---------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------- |
| Humility        | Kneel, bow, raise hands                  | "God is great. God is good. And God is near." |
| Responsibility | Open hands receiving, then drawing close | "Good stewards of God's grace."               |
| Acclimation    | 180-degree turn                          | "People like us do things like this."         |
| Imitation      | Open hands extending outward             | "We so love the world that we give."          |
| Mission        | Arms wide, gathering                     | "Our story for God's glory."                  |

Each gauge has a prerequisite: Worship is foundational. Responsibility builds on it. Acclimation requires both. Imitation requires all three. Mission requires all four.

## Humility as the Foundation

Humility is not one practice among many — it is the support structure for everything else. "Humility supports the weight of all the others." (source: [2019-09-26-the-life-gauges-have-a-priority.md](../raw/2019-09-26-the-life-gauges-have-a-priority.md))

The sequence from Matthew 4:19 is diagnostic: "Come, follow me" precedes "I will make you fishers of men." Formation (following) comes before expression (fishing). The greatest concern for a lifetime must be the posture of worship and connection to Jesus — not output, not impact, not even [[mission]].

"As we encounter him, He forms us. And we express."

## Hierarchical Dependency

The gauges are not independent dials to be adjusted separately — they are hierarchically dependent (source: [2019-09-26-the-life-gauges-have-a-priority.md](../raw/2019-09-26-the-life-gauges-have-a-priority.md)):

- Humility without responsibility means passive spirituality
- Responsibility without acclimation means effort without transformed identity
- Acclimation without imitation means new identity without generosity
- Imitation without mission means love that turns inward

Each gauge, fully inhabited, opens onto the next. A person stuck at responsibility (right effort, wrong identity) needs acclimation — the turn toward Kingdom culture — before mission becomes coherent.

## The Postures

Physical postures embody each gauge (source: [2019-10-14-the-life-gauges-through-postures.md](../raw/2019-10-14-the-life-gauges-through-postures.md)):

- **Humility**: fall to knees, bow, raise hands — "thoroughly defeated by overwhelming love"
- **Responsibility**: extended open hands receiving, then drawing to body — receiving grace, taking stewardship
- **Acclimation**: 180-degree turn — rejecting worldly messages, accepting God's truth (ongoing, not one-time)
- **Imitation**: reverse of responsibility — open hands extending outward — generosity from what was received; "freely we have received, now we freely give"
- **Mission**: arms extended as a bear hug — gathering, "corralling people to a place of acceptance and shalom"

The physical practice reinforces the spiritual intention. The body learns what the mind intends; posture is pedagogy.

## Grace as the Enabling Force

All five gauges operate through the Holy Spirit. Grace is defined as "unmerited God-given desire, ability, and resources to participate in God's economy as his beloved." Formation through the gauges is not self-improvement; it is cooperation with grace.

The relevant scriptural anchors (source: [2019-08-17-summarizing-the-life-gauges.md](../raw/2019-08-17-summarizing-the-life-gauges.md)) span Romans, Galatians, Ephesians, Philippians, and the Gospels — the framework is not a standalone model but a synthesis of pervasive New Testament themes.
