# Wisdom

**Summary**: Wisdom in the blog is distinguished from intelligence and information — it requires humility before God, contextual discernment, and the interpretive key of Jesus; it cannot be manufactured by processing power alone.

## Wisdom vs. Intelligence

As artificial intelligence advances, the blog draws a sharp distinction between intelligence (processing and pattern-matching) and wisdom (source: [2025-07-03-ai.md](../raw/2025-07-03-ai.md)):

> "Machines may replace human labor. They cannot replace wisdom."

Wisdom requires knowing *and humbling oneself before* the Holy One. Proverbs 9:10 ("The fear of the Lord is the beginning of wisdom") makes reverence a prerequisite — something no algorithm can have. Intelligence without wisdom is competence without character; it produces capability but not [[knowing God]], and it cannot generate the knowledge that constitutes eternal life.

## The Interpretive Key

Using the same scriptural texts, people have produced Mother Teresa and the Crusades, communities of hospitality and communities of violence. The difference is not intelligence applied to Scripture but whether Jesus is used as the interpretive key (source: [2025-06-17-the-interpretive-key.md](../raw/2025-06-17-the-interpretive-key.md)):

> "Jesus came to reveal what God is truly like and what a human being should be like."

A Christocentric hermeneutic — "all eyes on Jesus" — does not resolve every difficult passage, but it orients the reader toward the God revealed in Christ rather than toward whatever the reader's culture or interests might prefer to find.

## Discerning the Climate

Wisdom requires contextual reading — discerning the conditions in which a text or action occurs. "We need to discern the climate" (source: [2025-07-27-climate.md](../raw/2025-07-27-climate.md)). Like humidity that means different things at different temperatures ("sweater or swelter?"), the same principle plays out differently across historical and cultural climates.

Paul's first-century efforts to elevate low-status people read differently in the 21st century if context is stripped away. Wise interpretation holds the original climate alongside the contemporary one rather than flattening both into a universal abstraction.

## Wisdom as Formation Goal

The [[spiritual formation]] framework frames formation not only as ethical improvement but epistemological transformation — learning to *know* as Christ knows (source: [2025-09-15-trained-by-christ.md](../raw/2025-09-15-trained-by-christ.md)). Jesus' own pattern was to act only from what he sees the Father doing (John 5:19). Being trained in wisdom is being trained in this kind of knowing-before-acting.

The four formative questions of [[spiritual formation]] — Who is God? How does he dwell? Who are we in Christ? What is the Kingdom like? — are essentially wisdom-formation questions, directing attention toward the knowledge that makes all other knowledge coherent.

## Wisdom in Community

Healthy [[community]] is characterized by *knowledge* as well as *goodness and competence* (Romans 15:14). Peer-to-peer instruction distributes wisdom rather than concentrating it in a single teacher (source: [2025-09-14-promising-culture.md](../raw/2025-09-14-promising-culture.md)). The community's collective discernment is a form of wisdom that exceeds any individual's.
