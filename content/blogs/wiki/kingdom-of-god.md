## The First Way

Christianity is commonly called "the third way" — neither Left nor Right. The blog improves on this framing: Christianity is the **first way**, humanity's original design before sin scattered us into competing tribes (source: [2026-04-07-the-first-way.md](../raw/2026-04-07-the-first-way.md)).

"Holy" means *set apart, called out* — a distinct identity rooted in God's kingdom under Christ's [[authority]]. [[Repentance]] is the return to this first way; Jesus is both the way and the way-maker.

Treating Christianity as merely "a third option" between existing political camps keeps it reactive. Treating it as the first way positions it as the original, the standard, the invitation back to what human life was designed to be.

## The Right-Side-Up Kingdom

The Kingdom appears "upside down" only by the world's metrics. By God's metrics, it is the right-side-up one (source: [2025-11-12-upside-down-kingdom.md](../raw/2025-11-12-upside-down-kingdom.md)). The Beatitudes (Matthew 5) map Kingdom favor:

- The humble, not the powerful
- The mourning, not the comfortable
- The gentle, not the aggressive
- Those hungering for justice, not those who already have it
- The merciful, peacemakers, and oppressed

Paul echoes these values in 1 Corinthians 13 and Galatians 5. Kingdom culture is consistently self-giving toward God and neighbor, not self-advancing.

## Vision, Mission, and Strategy

The Kingdom provides a complete framework for church and Christian life (source: [2020-01-15-vision-mission-and-strategy.md](../raw/2020-01-15-vision-mission-and-strategy.md)):

- **Vision** (what we're moving toward): Revelation 21 — the new creation, God dwelling with people, all tears wiped away
- **Mission** (what we're doing now): Luke 4 / Isaiah 61 — good news to the poor, release for captives, sight for the blind, freedom for the oppressed
- **Strategy** (how we do it): Matthew 22 + 28 — love God, love neighbor, make disciples

This tests contemporary church activity against scriptural foundations. Any vision, mission, or strategy that departs from these anchors has drifted from the Kingdom.

## Kingdom Cultural Fluency

[[Discipleship]] is measured by Kingdom cultural fluency — becoming so immersed in Kingdom culture that its norms, values, and expectations feel native rather than foreign (source: [2019-08-13-cultural-literacy-and-fluency.md](../raw/2019-08-13-cultural-literacy-and-fluency.md)). The four formative questions of [[spiritual formation]] are tools for developing this fluency:

1. Who is God?
2. How does God dwell with his people?
3. Who are we in Christ?
4. What is God's kingdom like?

The phrase "People like us do things like this" captures Kingdom cultural identity: *who we are* (in Christ) shapes *how we live* (Kingdom norms).

## The Church as Royal Priesthood

"What is the Church, if not a Royal Priesthood and an embodiment of the Royal Law?" (source: [2020-10-31-together-we-are.md](../raw/2020-10-31-together-we-are.md), citing 1 Peter 2:9 and James 2:8)

The Church is a community that embodies the Kingdom's Royal Law — "Love your neighbor as yourself" — in its common life. This is not metaphor; it is the actual social form the Kingdom takes in the world.
