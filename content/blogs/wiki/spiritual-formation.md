# Spiritual Formation

**Summary**: Spiritual formation is the lifelong process of being shaped into Christlikeness through structured habits, attentive postures, and the work of the Holy Spirit — the means by which [[knowing God]] becomes a lived reality.

**Sources**: [2020-03-28-four-asks-of-formation.md](../raw/2020-03-28-four-asks-of-formation.md), [2019-08-17-summarizing-the-life-gauges.md](../raw/2019-08-17-summarizing-the-life-gauges.md), [2019-10-14-the-life-gauges-through-postures.md](../raw/2019-10-14-the-life-gauges-through-postures.md), [2020-03-04-three-holy-habits.md](../raw/2020-03-04-three-holy-habits.md), [2025-10-29-spaces-of-change.md](../raw/2025-10-29-spaces-of-change.md), [2025-08-14-deconstruction.md](../raw/2025-08-14-deconstruction.md), [2017-10-01-creating-unsafe-spaces.md](../raw/2017-10-01-creating-unsafe-spaces.md), [2021-05-13-effectiveness.md](../raw/2021-05-13-effectiveness.md)

## Four Formative Questions

The most concentrated description of formation asks four questions that should shape all scripture reading and Christian learning (source: [2020-03-28-four-asks-of-formation.md](../raw/2020-03-28-four-asks-of-formation.md)):

1. **Who is God?** What is he like? What do his names reveal? How does he reveal himself?
2. **How does God dwell with his people?** The patterns of guidance, correction, and care — from OT tabernacle to Christ to Spirit to Revelation 21.
3. **Who are we in Christ?** Being *immersed in* Jesus' ways and *saturated with* them — resulting in a new identity.
4. **What is God's Kingdom like?** Its culture described as "upside-down (or right-side-up)" — the opposite of worldly patterns.

Through this habit of questioning, guided by the Holy Spirit, people are "formed more and more into the likeness of Jesus."

## Formation and the Holy Spirit

Formation is not self-improvement. The [[life gauges]] framework is explicit: "All spiritual growth operates through the Holy Spirit, not human effort alone." Grace is "unmerited God-given desire, ability, and resources to participate in God's economy." The Spirit is the agent; practices and habits are the conditions of receptivity.

## Spaces of Change

Spiritual growth involves seasons of genuine shift — not incremental improvement but actual movement into "bigger spaces, not smaller" (source: [2025-10-29-spaces-of-change.md](../raw/2025-10-29-spaces-of-change.md)). The scaffolding is Philippians 1:4-6: "He who began a good work in you will carry it on to completion."

Formation is not linear or predictable. It includes disruption, doubt, and the unsettling of old certainties. The promise is continuity of God's work even through the disruptions.

## Deconstruction as Restoration

When beliefs or structures need to come apart, the right frame is deconstruction rather than destruction (source: [2025-08-14-deconstruction.md](../raw/2025-08-14-deconstruction.md)):

- **Destruction**: rubble, nothing preserved, no rebuilding possible
- **Deconstruction**: careful dismantling that sets aside and preserves everything reusable, oriented toward rebuilding on a better foundation

God is a restorer who gathers remnants. Spiritual deconstruction, rightly understood, is a means of formation — not its enemy. The danger is when deconstruction becomes destruction: tearing down with no intention to build.

## Unsafe Spaces

Genuine formation is not comfortable. Leaders are called to carve out "unsafe spaces" — not dangerous but challenging — where people encounter Jesus in ways that demand they see differently (source: [2017-10-01-creating-unsafe-spaces.md](../raw/2017-10-01-creating-unsafe-spaces.md)). Jesus' cleansing of the Temple is the image: *MAY I PLEASE HAVE YOUR ATTENTION?* Formation requires the willingness to be disrupted.

## Living in the Gospel's Effectiveness

A practical test of formation: does a person actually live as if the Gospel made a difference? The blog critiques the contradiction of affirming Christ's effectiveness while living in guilt, shame, and fear (source: [2021-05-13-effectiveness.md](../raw/2021-05-13-effectiveness.md)). If Christ's death was completely effective — moving believers from guilt to innocence, shame to honor, fear to spiritual power — then formation means learning to inhabit that new reality, not just agree with it theologically.
